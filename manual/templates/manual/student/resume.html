The 'Resumes' page is a portal that allows you to manage your resumes.

Resumes are virtual files that are sent to companies when you apply for a job post. They should contain your education, work experience and other information important to employers.

  • To create a new resume, click on the ‘New Resume’ button and go through the walkthrough adding your information as you go.
    • If you already have a resume, your education and languages will be added in automatically.
    • You can also choose to copy an existing resume and modify it from there.
  • To edit a resume, click on the name of that resume. (Note: any changes made to the resume are automatically updated so that companies which have that resume on file for an application can view your latest changes)
  • To remove a resume click on the delete link for that resume. (Note: all applications that were made with this resume will still have it)
  • To upload a transcript to your resume (must be a pdf) go to the resume details page and click on the upload transcript button.
  • To activate a resume click on the ‘make active’ link. (Note: the active resume is the one that is sent when you apply for a job post)
  • To get an overview of what your resume contains click on the name of that resume.
  • To quickly manage a section of your resume (Languages, Experience, etc…) go to the appropriate section and make your changes (add, edit or delete).
A resume is composed of six sections:
  • Education

    Education/School records represent your academic history. We recommend that you specify it at least back to your high school education.

    • To add a new school to your resume click on the ‘New School’ button. (Note: you can add in school records that are present in other resumes, but not the current one automatically)
    • To edit a school record click on the edit link for that school. (Note: any resumes containing this school record will be updated automatically so that companies can view your latest resumes)
    • To delete a school record click on the delete link for that school. (Note: this will only remove the school from the current resume)
  • Experience

    Experience records represent your work experience.

    • To add a new experience record to your resume click on the ‘New Experience’ button. (Note: you can add in experience records that are present in other resumes, but not the current one automatically)
    • To edit an experience record click on the edit link for that experience record. (Note: any resumes containing this experience record will be updated automatically so that companies can view your latest resumes)
    • To delete an experience record click on the delete link for that experience record. (Note: this will only remove the experience from the current resume)
  • Awards

    Awards represent any awards (academic, sports or other) that you won, but also represent any professional certificates that you acquired.

    • To add a new award to your resume click on the ‘New Award’ button. (Note: you can add in award records that are present in other resumes, but not the current one automatically)
    • To edit an award record click on the edit link for that award. (Note: any resumes containing this award record will be updated automatically so that companies can view your latest resumes)
    • To delete an award record click on the delete link for that award. (Note: this will only remove the award from the current resume)
  • Languages

    Language records represent the languages that you are familiar with.

    • To add a new language to your resume click on the ‘New Language’ button. (Note: you can add in language records that are present in other resumes, but not the current one automatically)
    • To edit a language record click on the edit link for that language. (Note: any resumes containing this language will be updated automatically so that companies can view your latest resumes)
    • To delete a language record click on the delete link for that language. (Note: this will only remove the language from the current resume)
  • Skills

    Skill records represent any skills that you have. (For example: photoshop knowledge, typing speed, photography, programming knowledge, etc…)

    • To add a new skill to your resume click on the ‘New Skill’ button. (Note: you can add in skill records that are present in other resumes, but not the current one automatically)
    • To edit a skill record click on the edit link for that skill. (Note: any resumes containing this skill record will be updated automatically so that companies can view your latest resumes)
    • To delete a skill record click on the delete link for that skill. (Note: this will only remove the skill from the current resume)
  • References

    Reference records represent any professional or academic references that you have. (For example: your employer, a teacher, a supervisor, a mentor, etc…)

    • To add a new reference to your resume click on the ‘New Reference’ button. (Note: you can add in reference records that are present in other resumes, but not the current one automatically)
    • To edit a reference record click on the edit link for that reference. (Note: any resumes containing this reference record will be updated automatically so that companies can view your latest resumes)
    • To delete a reference record click on the delete link for that reference. (Note: this will only remove the reference from the current resume)
