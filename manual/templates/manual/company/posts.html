{% endblock %} {% block howto %}

Posts

  • To create a new post click on the 'New Post' button.
  • To edit a post click on the 'Edit' link for that post.
  • To view a post's details click on the post's title in the table.
  • To deactivate a post, click on the 'close post' link in the post's detail page.
  • To reactivate an old post click on the 'Reactivate' link for that post in the expired posts section.
  • Use the pagination at the bottom if necessary to navigate through your posts.

Applications

  • To view a post's applicants click on the 'Applicants' link for that post.
  • To view an expired post's applicants click on the 'Applicants' link.
  • Take a look at the 'Legend' in the sidebar for color codes.
  • To view applicants in a list format click on the 'Multiple Applicants View'.
  • To view applicants one at a time click on the 'Single Applicant View'.
  • Navigate through applicants from the 'Single Applicant View' using the 'Next Applicant' and 'Previous Applicant' buttons.
  • To request a cover letter from an applicant, navigate to his/her detail page by clicking on their name and click on the 'Request Cover Letter' button at the bottom of the page.
  • To discard an application click on discard link for that applicant in the 'Multiple Applicants View' or click on the 'Discard Applicant' button in the 'Single Applicant View'.
  • To view an applicant's details from the 'Multiple Applicants View' click on his/her name in the table.
  • To download an applicant's PDF resume, go to his/her detail page and click on the 'Download PDF Resume' button at the bottom of the page.
Application Filters
  • To filter your list of applicants by schools, enter the names all the schools for which you would like to see the applicants.
  • To filter your list of applicants by majors, select all the majors for which you would like to see the applicants.
  • To filter your list of applicants by GPA (US GPA 0-4.0), enter the minimum GPA for which you would like to see the applicants.
  • You can combine all the previous by applying them as discussed respectively.
  • To remove a filter, click on the red ‘X’ next to it in the ‘Current filters’ section.
  • DANGER!
    You can discard all the applicants that do not fall in your filtered values by un-checking the 'Keep applications that are no in the filtered values' checkbox.
  • To clear the filter click on the 'Clear Filter' button.

Posts

  • Who can apply for my post?

    In the 'Post Form' you are allowed to restrict your post to a certain program. If that is the case, only students of that program will be able to apply to your post. Otherwise all students will be able to apply to your post. (Note: when you receive applications, you will be able to filter them by school, major (if you specified a program) or GPA)

  • How will I know when there is activity on a post?

    The notification next to the post will indicate the type of activity going on with a post. If there is no notification, that means there is no recent activity.

  • What happens after I edit a post?

    All the changes made will automatically be updated and shared across the Jobin platform.

  • What happens to applications when a post closes?

    All applications are kept for you to keep looking through, but you will not be able to receive new applications for that post unless you reactivate it.

  • When do I stop receiving new applications?

    You can stop new receiving new applications by closing the post. Otherwise, posts are no longer visible by candidates after the application deadline has passed.

Applications

  • What are expired posts?

    Expired posts serve three purposes:

    • To keep a record of your posts.
    • To give you access to the posts applicants even after the post has expired (Even though you will not receive any new applications for that post, all the ones you haven't discarded will still be accessible)
    • If you will be hiring for the same position in some time, reactivating an expired post allows you to recreate the same post without having to refill all the forms, just change the deadline, start and end dates and its live.
  • What happens after I request a cover letter?

    A notification is sent to the student and you can track the status by the color code for that application in the 'Applicants' view. Moreover, you will be notified when you receive a response.

  • How do I get in touch with a candidate?

    You can use the platform to request a cover letter to that applicant, for any further communication we provide you with the student's email address.

Application Filters

  • Where can I see the active filter?

    Active filters can be viewed in the 'Current filters' section.

The 'Posts' section allows you to manage your posts and their applications effectively.

Your posts represent the job offers that you created, available for the student users of the Jobin platform. Once these users apply, their applications are pooled together in one place where you can filter them to your liking to select the best possible candidate.

  • Posts

    Your post objects are what will allow you to reach the students using the Jobin platform. They will allow you to receive and sort through hopefully many applicants to find the right talent for your organization.

    • Create a new post by clicking on the 'New Post' button. (Note: to narrow the target candidates for a post, specify the program field by selecting the appropriate target program.)
    • Edit a post by clicking on the 'Edit' link of that post in the list. (Note: all changes made to a post are transmitted to applicants in real time)
    • View a post's details by clicking on the post's title in the list.
    • To close a post click on the 'Close Post' link in the post's detail view.
    • You can reactivate an expired post to save yourself the hassle of refilling the forms by clicking on the 'Reactivate' link of that post in the 'Expired Posts' section.

    NB: your posts remain visible to possible applicants until the application deadline, after which you cannot receive any new applications, but will still be able to browse through the applications that were already received.

  • Applicants

    Applicants are all the student's who chose to apply for your job post. You can look through them and filter them using set filters to find the ideal candidate.

    • To view a post's applicants click on the 'Applicants' link for that post, or from the detail view click on the 'View Post Applicants' button.
    • You can view applicants either in a list (Multiple Applicants View) or one by one (Single Applicant View). Use the buttons at the top of the page to go from one view to the other.
    • Applicants have notifications next to their names in the 'Multiple Applicants View' to let you quickly identify what the state of each application is.
    • The 'Single Applicant View' let's you navigate through the applicants one at a time, showing you their resume and other information they may have supplied. (Note: to navigate between applicants, use the previous/next button at the top of the page)
    • Actions related to applicants
      • To request a cover letter from an applicant click on the 'Request Cover Letter' button in the 'Single Applicant View' at the bottom of the page.
      • To download a pdf version of the applicant's resume click on the 'Download PDF Resume' button at the bottom of the page in the 'Single Applicant View'
      • To discard an application click on the 'Discard' link in the 'Multiple Applicants View' or use the 'Discard Applicant' button in the 'Single Applicant View' at the bottom of the page.
      • To contact an applicant, use his email address that is provided with the resume.
  • Applicant Filters

    The applicant filters are available in the 'Multiple Applicants View' to help you narrow down your list of applicants.

    • School Filter
      The school filter lets you filter the applicants by school. (Note: you can select more than one school)
    • Major Filter
      The major filter lets you filter the applicants by major. (Note: you can select more than one major.)
    • GPA Filter
      The GPA filter lets you filter the applicants by GPA. The GPA is noted on a scale from 0-4.0 as per the American system. (Note: the GPA filter will return all the applicants with a GPA greater than or equal to the value entered)
    • You can combine all the filters together to get a more specific pool of candidates.
    • Danger!
      You can also chose to discard all the applications that do not match the filter criteria, but be careful as this action is irreversible. We suggest that you apply the filter first without discarding the applicants; once you are satisfied with the filtered results you can proceed to discarding the other applicants.
